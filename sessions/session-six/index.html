Chapter 1 — What Is a Prompt?

Understand the fundamental building block of AI communication and why learning to write better prompts changes everything.

What is a Prompt?

A prompt is any instruction or input you give to an AI model. Every interaction you have with an AI starts with a prompt — whether you type a single word or a full paragraph.

  • Explain gravity.
  • Write an email to my boss.
  • Summarize this 10-page article.
  • Teach me Python in 30 days.

What is Prompt Engineering?

Prompt Engineering is the skill of designing instructions for AI systems that consistently produce useful, accurate, and high-quality outputs.

Core Principle

Better instructions always lead to better outputs. The AI doesn't improve — you improve.

Why Does It Matter?

The same AI model will give dramatically different answers depending on how you ask. One poorly written prompt can waste hours. One well-crafted prompt can save days.

✕ Weak Prompt

Tell me about exercise.

✓ Strong Prompt

Design a 4-week muscle-building workout plan for a 17-year-old beginner with access only to resistance bands and bodyweight exercises.

Myth vs Reality

Myth

There are secret magic prompts that unlock AI's full power.

Reality

Clarity, specificity, and context beat any trick. Clear thinking produces clear prompts.

Prompt Engineering is really just clear thinking made visible.

Chapter 2 — How AI Understands Your Prompt

Understand what happens inside the model when it reads your prompt — and why vague inputs always produce vague outputs.

AI Has No Mind-Reading Ability

The model works exclusively with what you provide. It has no access to your intentions, background knowledge, or unstated goals. Everything it needs to answer you well must be in the prompt.

The Golden Rule

If it's not in the prompt, the AI doesn't know it.

Garbage In, Garbage Out

Vague

Help me.

Specific

Help me create a 7-day study plan for my biology exam covering chapters 3-7, with 2-hour daily sessions and built-in review periods.

Specific Beats General

Too broad

Tell me about history.

Focused

Explain the 3 main causes of World War I to a 10th-grader using analogies and bullet points.

Prompting is a Conversation

You rarely need a perfect prompt on the first try. The most effective approach is iterative:

  1. Ask an initial question
  2. Evaluate what's missing or off
  3. Add more context or constraints
  4. Refine until the output is what you need
Pro Tip

After any AI response, you can say "That's good, but make it shorter," or "Add more examples," or "Rewrite this for a 12-year-old." Follow-up prompts are just as powerful as first prompts.

Chapter 3 — The Formula of Great Prompts

Every consistently great prompt contains five ingredients. Learn the formula and you'll never write a bad prompt again.

The Golden Formula
Task
+ Context
+ Constraints
+ Format
+ Tone
─────────
= Consistent Results

1. Task — What Do You Want?

The action you want the AI to perform. Start with a strong verb.

  • Write / Create / Draft
  • Explain / Teach / Summarize
  • Analyze / Compare / Evaluate
  • Generate / List / Brainstorm

2. Context — Why Do You Need It?

Background information that helps the AI produce the right answer for your specific situation.

Example

"I'm a junior developer at a SaaS startup. We use React and TypeScript. Our codebase follows atomic design principles."

3. Constraints — What Are the Limits?

  • Word count: "Under 150 words"
  • Audience: "For a non-technical CEO"
  • Time: "Achievable in 30 days"
  • Scope: "Only about organic SEO, not paid ads"

4. Format — How Should It Look?

  • Bullet points / Numbered list
  • Table / Comparison chart
  • Step-by-step guide
  • JSON / Code block / Markdown
  • Essay / Report / Email

5. Tone — What Voice?

  • Professional / Formal
  • Casual / Conversational
  • Simple / Child-friendly
  • Technical / Academic
  • Persuasive / Marketing
Example — All Five Elements

Task: Write a product description. Context: For an eco-friendly reusable water bottle targeting Gen Z. Constraints: Under 100 words, no clichés. Format: 2 paragraphs. Tone: Authentic and slightly edgy.

Chapter 4 — Practical Prompting Techniques

Master these proven techniques to unlock dramatically better outputs from any AI model.

Role Prompting

Assigning a persona or role to the AI radically improves the quality of its responses in that domain.

Examples

"Act as a senior software engineer with 10 years of Python experience reviewing my code."

"You are a Harvard-trained nutritionist. A client asks you..."

Chain of Thought (CoT)

Asking the AI to think step-by-step dramatically improves accuracy on complex reasoning tasks.

Without CoT

What is 17% of 340?

With CoT

What is 17% of 340? Think step-by-step and show your reasoning.

Few-Shot Prompting

Giving the AI 2–3 examples of what you want before asking for the real output.

Template

"Here are two examples of how I want the output formatted: [Example 1] [Example 2]. Now do the same for: [Your actual request]"

Step-by-Step Prompting

One shot

Teach me how to invest in stocks.

Progressive

Teach me stock market investing step-by-step, starting from "what is a stock" and building up to portfolio construction. I'm a complete beginner.

Follow-Up Prompts

After any response, you can refine with:

  • "Make this 50% shorter."
  • "Add 3 concrete examples."
  • "Rewrite this for a 12-year-old."
  • "Now make it more persuasive."
  • "What are the counterarguments?"
  • "Format this as a table."

Breaking Big Tasks

Instead of one huge prompt, break complex projects into atomic steps. This reduces errors and keeps responses focused.

Strategy

Step 1: "Outline the structure of a business plan for a coffee shop." → Step 2: "Now write section 1: Executive Summary." → Step 3: "Now write section 2: Market Analysis."

Chapter 5 — Learning With AI

Transform AI into your personal tutor, study partner, quiz master, and language coach.

AI as a Personal Tutor

AI can adapt explanations to any knowledge level, any style, and any pace. Unlike YouTube videos or textbooks, it responds to your specific confusion.

Use Cases

  • Concept explanation: "Explain photosynthesis using a cooking analogy."
  • Quiz generation: "Create 10 multiple-choice questions on Chapter 4 of my biology textbook."
  • Spaced repetition: "Quiz me on Spanish vocabulary. After each answer, tell me if I was right and why."
  • Simplification: "Explain quantum entanglement as if I'm 10 years old."
  • Deep dive: "I understand X. Now take me one level deeper."
Best Practice

Tell the AI your current knowledge level at the start: "I know basic algebra but have never studied calculus."

Language Learning

  • "Talk to me in French. Correct my grammar mistakes in brackets."
  • "Translate this sentence to Japanese and explain the grammar structure."
  • "Give me 20 common Spanish phrases for traveling. Include pronunciation guides."

Chapter 6 — Writing With AI

Use AI to draft, rewrite, summarize, and brainstorm across every type of written content.

Writing From Scratch

  • Emails (professional, cold outreach, apology, follow-up)
  • Essays and academic papers
  • Blog posts and articles
  • Social media captions
  • Presentation scripts

Rewriting & Improving

Paste your text and ask AI to improve it in specific ways:

  • "Fix grammar and spelling without changing the tone."
  • "Make this more concise — cut it by 40%."
  • "Make this sound more professional."
  • "Add more emotional impact to this paragraph."

Summarizing

  • Long research papers → 5 key bullet points
  • Meeting notes → action items
  • Books → chapter summaries
  • YouTube transcripts → key takeaways

Brainstorming

  • "Give me 20 blog post ideas about productivity for remote workers."
  • "Generate 10 creative names for a sustainable clothing brand."
  • "What are 15 angles I could write about cryptocurrency that haven't been done to death?"
Important

Always review AI-written content. Verify facts, add your personal voice, and ensure it represents your actual views.

Chapter 7 — Research With AI

Accelerate research without sacrificing accuracy. Learn when to trust AI and when to verify.

What AI Is Good At for Research

  • Explaining concepts and background knowledge quickly
  • Identifying key questions to research
  • Summarizing articles you paste in
  • Comparing multiple perspectives on a topic
  • Structuring research into an outline

Best Research Tools

  • Perplexity AI: Best for finding sources and citations
  • ChatGPT: Best for understanding concepts
  • Google Scholar + Claude: Paste papers, ask questions
  • Elicit / Consensus: AI-powered academic paper search
Critical Rule

AI can hallucinate facts, statistics, and citations. NEVER cite AI-generated statistics in academic or professional work without independent verification.

The Research Workflow

  1. Use AI to get background understanding
  2. Use Perplexity or Scholar for sourced facts
  3. Paste real papers into Claude for analysis
  4. Use AI to help structure your findings
  5. Write your own conclusions based on verified sources

Chapter 9 — Common Mistakes

Avoid these 8 mistakes that beginner and intermediate prompters make constantly.

1. Being Too Vague

Mistake

Tell me about business.

Fix

Explain the 5 most common reasons startups fail in their first year, with one real-world example each.

2. Asking Too Much at Once

Break large tasks into steps. Asking AI to "write a complete business plan, financial model, and marketing strategy" in one prompt produces shallow results for all three.

3. Providing No Context

The AI doesn't know your age, profession, knowledge level, goals, or constraints unless you say so. Always add relevant context.

4. Blind Trust in Outputs

AI is confidently wrong sometimes. Always verify important facts, statistics, and technical details from primary sources.

5. Quitting After One Prompt

The first response is a starting point. Expert prompters iterate 3-5 times on average.

6. Ignoring the Tone Mismatch

AI's default tone is academic and generic. Always specify the tone you need.

7. Not Using Constraints

Without limits, AI writes to fill space. Specify length, format, and scope.

8. Over-Complicating Prompts

More words ≠ better results. Focus on clarity, not length. One clear sentence often beats five rambling ones.

The goal is not to outsmart the AI. The goal is to communicate with it clearly.
