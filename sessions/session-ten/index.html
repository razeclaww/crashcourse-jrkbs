Earning Online:
Tutoring & Freelancing

A practical guide for Nepali teachers (Grades 1–8) to earn extra income using your skills, internet, and simple tools.

This guide covers online tutoring via Google Meet, local freelancing with Canva, creating ebooks, and finding work on Nepali freelance platforms.

Start Reading ↓

1 · Online Tutoring (Video Call Classes)

You can teach students from Grades 1–8 online — your own students, neighbours, or anyone interested. Use your mobile or laptop, a good internet connection, and a video conferencing app.

What you need to start

  • A computer or mobile with a camera and microphone.
  • Reliable internet (for smooth video calls).
  • A quiet room with a clean background.
  • Video conferencing app — we recommend Google Meet (it's free and works with Gmail).

Google Meet (Simple & Free)

Google Meet is the most popular and reliable tool in Nepal. You just need a Gmail account. No downloads required.

How to use Google Meet

1. Open your Gmail.
2. Click on the 9 dots (Google apps) and select "Meet".
3. Click "New meeting" → "Create a meeting for later".
4. Copy the meeting link and share it via WhatsApp or Facebook.
5. At class time, join the meeting, then click "Present now" to share your screen (notes, PDFs, whiteboard).
6. Use mute/unmute, chat for questions, and you can even record the class.

You can also use Zoom or Microsoft Teams, but Google Meet is the easiest for most Nepali teachers because everyone already has a Gmail.

How much to charge?

In Nepal, rates vary. For Grades 1–8, you can charge Rs. 500 – Rs. 800 per hour for one-on-one classes. For group classes (2–5 students), charge Rs. 300–400 per student per hour.

Real idea

Offer "exam preparation special classes" in your school's WhatsApp group. Many parents look for extra help during Dashain/Tihar holidays and before final exams.

2 · Local Freelancing (Digital Services)

You don't need to be a professional designer. With Canva (free) and basic computer skills, you can offer design services to local businesses — right in your neighbourhood.

What you can create

  • Posters & banners for restaurants, tuition centres, or local events.
  • Menu cards for small eateries and cafes.
  • ID cards for schools, clubs, or cooperatives.
  • Nepali calendars with dates and festivals — businesses often sponsor these.
  • Invitation cards for weddings, bratabandha, or parties.

Canva (Free & Powerful)

canva.com
Best for: All kinds of designs

Canva has thousands of templates — posters, flyers, menus, ID cards, calendars. The free version is enough. Just design, export as PDF/PNG, and share.

Tips:
  • Visit a local shop and say, "I can create a digital menu/poster for you."
  • Price: Rs. 200–500 per design.
  • For calendars, charge Rs. 500–1000 (even more if you get a sponsor).

Facebook Marketplace & WhatsApp Groups

—
Best for: Finding clients

Post your designs on Facebook, especially in local community groups. Share your work on WhatsApp status. Word of mouth works well in Nepal.

Real approach:
  • Walk into nearby stationery shops, tailor shops, or salons and offer your service.
  • Make 1–2 free samples first to show your quality, then charge.
  • Local clubs and schools often need annual calendars — offer them a deal.
A genuine opportunity

Digital wedding / bratabandha invitations — there are many ceremonies in Nepal. Design a beautiful digital invite, send it via WhatsApp. Charge Rs. 300–500 per card. There's real demand for this.

3 · Ebooks & Guide Books

You have years of teaching notes, question banks, and worksheets. Compile them into a simple, easy-to-read guide book. Students and parents will pay for it.

What kind of books can you make?

  • Subject guides: Class 8 Science, Class 5 English Grammar, Class 7 Math Shortcuts, etc.
  • Exam preparation books: "Final Exam Tips for Class 8" — with important questions and practice tests.
  • Activity books: For younger students (Grades 1–3) — fun worksheets, coloring, and puzzles.
  • Teacher's planner / lesson plan templates: Useful for new teachers.

How to create your book

Step 1: Collect your notes, worksheets, and questions from the last few years.
Step 2: Type everything in Google Docs or Microsoft Word. Use simple language, bullet points, and examples.
Step 3: Design a cover page using Canva (make it attractive).
Step 4: Export as PDF.
Step 5: Start selling — via WhatsApp, Facebook, or even your local stationery shop.

Pricing & selling ideas

PDF Ebook: Rs. 100–300 per copy (sent via WhatsApp).
Printed book: Print 20–50 copies at a local print shop (cost around Rs. 150–200 per book). Sell for Rs. 300–500 each.
School tie-up: Suggest your book as a reference guide to parents in your school. They'll buy it.

Real success story

A teacher in Kathmandu made a "Class 8 Science Shortcuts" ebook — just 50 pages. She sold it for Rs. 150 on WhatsApp and has already sold over 200 copies. Students loved it because it made revision easy. You can do the same for any subject.

4 · Freelance Platforms in Nepal

You don't always have to knock on doors. Several Nepali websites let you find teaching and design work online. Here's how they work.

PayoWork

payowork.com
Best for: General freelancing & tutoring in Nepal

PayoWork is a homegrown Nepali platform where you can offer your services — from tutoring and writing to design. It connects local clients with local freelancers.

How it works:
  • Sign up and complete your profile.
  • Browse available projects or post your service.
  • Submit a proposal to a client.
  • Get hired, do the work, and receive payment via local bank transfer.

Swadhin

swadhin.com
Best for: Building a freelancer network

Swadhin aims to create a freelancing culture in Nepal. It's a community where skilled individuals can find work and build their careers.

How it works:
  • Create a detailed profile highlighting your teaching or design skills.
  • Businesses looking for Nepali talent will discover you.
  • Clients can contact you directly for projects.
  • Discuss rates and timelines directly with the client.

OnlineKaam

onlinekaam.com
Best for: Verified freelancers & startups

Described as Nepal's first digital employment marketplace, OnlineKaam connects startups and businesses with verified freelancers.

How it works:
  • Register and get your skills verified.
  • Browse the listed job opportunities.
  • Apply for projects that match your skills.
  • Complete the project and get paid via bank transfer or eSewa.

WorkBridge

workbridge.com.np
Best for: Students & beginners gaining experience

WorkBridge is designed specifically for Nepali students and beginners who want to gain industry-level experience by working on real-world projects.

How it works:
  • Sign up and take a quick skill test.
  • Startups post short, entry-level projects.
  • Complete the project, get feedback, and build your portfolio.
  • Great for building experience before taking on bigger clients.

Kumari Job

kumarijob.com
Best for: Regular job listings, including teaching

Kumari Job is a well-known Nepali job portal that has a dedicated section for education. You can find full-time, part-time, and online tutoring jobs here.

How it works:
  • Search for "online tutor" or "home tuition" in the search bar.
  • Filter by location (or choose "remote").
  • Click "Apply" and submit your CV.
  • Schools and training centres will contact you for interviews.

MeroJob / NecoJobs

merojob.com
Best for: Finding online teaching positions

MeroJob (and similar sites like NecoJobs) regularly post online tutoring jobs from schools, colleges, and educational companies across Nepal.

How it works:
  • Create an account and upload your CV.
  • Search for "tutor", "online faculty", or "teacher".
  • Apply directly to job listings.
  • Many of these are remote or hybrid positions.
International Platforms (Bonus)

If you want to earn in dollars, you can also try international platforms like Upwork, Fiverr, and Freelancer. They accept Nepali freelancers. You'll need to set up a Payoneer or PayPal account to receive payments. Start with local platforms first to build your profile and confidence.

Quick tip

On any platform, your profile is your shop window. Add a professional photo, list your teaching experience, mention your best subjects (e.g., Math, English, Science), and include a sample of your work (like a lesson plan or a flyer you made). A good profile gets more clients.
