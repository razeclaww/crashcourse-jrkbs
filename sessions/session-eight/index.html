AI for Writing & Presentations

A Beginner's Guide for Teachers • Grades 1–8 • Mixed Subjects • Practical & Ready‑to‑Use

This guide helps you feel comfortable using AI for all your writing and presentation needs — lesson plans, emails, reports, student feedback, slide decks, speaker notes, and more. No tech jargon, just real tools, real prompts, and real results.

Start Reading ↓

1 · AI Tools for Writing

Whether you're drafting lesson plans, writing parent emails, creating student reports, or developing classroom materials — these AI tools will save you time and improve your writing quality. They help with brainstorming, structuring, editing, and polishing any text you need.

ChatGPT

chat.openai.com
Best for: Drafting, brainstorming, rewriting any text

Your go‑to AI writing assistant. You can ask it to write a first draft of a lesson plan, rewrite a parent email in a friendlier tone, or generate discussion questions. It adapts to your style and audience.

Quick tips:
  • Ask it to 'write in a warm, professional tone'
  • Use 'rewrite this for a Grade 3 audience' to simplify
  • Perfect for generating multiple versions of the same text

Grammarly

grammarly.com
Best for: Editing, grammar, tone, and clarity

Grammarly goes beyond spell‑checking — it suggests style improvements, adjusts tone (formal, friendly, confident), and helps you write more clearly. It works in your browser, Word, and Google Docs.

Quick tips:
  • Set a goal: 'Audience: Parents' for more empathetic suggestions
  • Use the tone detector to make sure your message lands right
  • Great for proofreading student feedback before sending

QuillBot

quillbot.com
Best for: Paraphrasing, summarising, and rephrasing

Sometimes you have a sentence that just doesn't sound right. QuillBot gives you multiple ways to rewrite it. It also has a summariser that can condense long articles into key points — perfect for creating study guides.

Quick tips:
  • Use the 'Synonyms' slider to control how much it changes
  • Summarise professional articles into plain English for students
  • Helps avoid repetitive language in your writing

Jasper

jasper.ai
Best for: Long‑form content, lesson plans, curriculum writing

Jasper is built for creating structured content. You give it a topic and audience, and it generates a full draft — from lesson plans to newsletters to blog posts. It has templates specifically for education.

Quick tips:
  • Use the 'Lesson Plan' template to save time
  • Set the tone to 'educational and engaging'
  • Great for curriculum development or grant writing

Copy.ai

copy.ai
Best for: Quick, creative writing and idea generation

If you're stuck for words, Copy.ai can generate dozens of ideas in seconds. It's excellent for coming up with creative hooks, parent survey questions, student writing prompts, or engaging subject introductions.

Quick tips:
  • Give it a simple prompt like 'write 10 hooks for a science lesson'
  • Use it for brainstorming before you write the full version
  • Great for social media posts or class updates

Writesonic

writesonic.com
Best for: Repurposing content and generating multiple versions

Writesonic can take a single idea and turn it into a lesson plan, a parent newsletter, a student handout, and a presentation outline — all in one go. It's perfect for teachers who want to create different formats from the same content.

Quick tips:
  • Use 'Article Rewriter' to adapt content for different audiences
  • Generate a set of questions from a text in seconds
  • Ideal for creating differentiated materials

2 · AI Tools for Presentations

Creating engaging slides and presentations can be time‑consuming. These AI tools help you design beautiful slides, generate speaker notes, structure your content, and even create entire presentations from a simple prompt — so you can focus on delivering your message.

Canva Magic Design

canva.com
Best for: Beautiful slide design and visual storytelling

Canva's AI (Magic Design) can turn a simple prompt into a complete presentation with layouts, images, and fonts that match. You can also upload a document and it will transform it into slides. Perfect for teachers who want professional‑looking presentations without design skills.

Quick tips:
  • Type a topic and get a full slide deck in seconds
  • Easily change themes and colours
  • Great for student‑facing presentations

Tome

tome.app
Best for: AI‑generated storytelling presentations

Tome is built for narrative presentations. You give it a prompt, and it generates a story‑like presentation with text, images, and a logical flow. It's excellent for lesson introductions, project showcases, and interactive student presentations.

Quick tips:
  • Prompt it with a topic and it creates a full narrative
  • Add images automatically from its library
  • Great for engaging class starter presentations

Gamma

gamma.app
Best for: Clean, modern presentations from any content

Gamma turns your text, notes, or PDFs into a polished presentation in minutes. It understands structure and creates clear, visually appealing slides with minimal effort. It's ideal when you have existing content that you want to present.

Quick tips:
  • Paste your lesson outline and get a presentation
  • Customise the look with one click
  • Works well for staff meetings or class lessons

Beautiful.ai

beautiful.ai
Best for: Designer‑quality slides with zero design effort

This tool uses AI to automatically layout your content in the most visually pleasing way. You just add text and images, and it arranges everything perfectly. It's like having a professional designer on your team.

Quick tips:
  • Add content and let the AI handle layout
  • Use smart templates tailored for education
  • Great for sharing visually with students and parents

Copilot for PowerPoint

microsoft.com
Best for: PowerPoint users who want AI‑assisted slide creation

Integrated into Microsoft 365, Copilot can turn a prompt into a full PowerPoint presentation complete with slides, speaker notes, and design suggestions. You can also ask it to rewrite or restructure existing slides.

Quick tips:
  • Type a topic and get a full deck
  • Ask it to add speaker notes to each slide
  • Perfect for those already using Microsoft

Prezi AI

prezi.com
Best for: Dynamic, non‑linear presentations

Prezi's AI helps you create interactive, zoomable presentations that are more engaging than traditional slides. It can suggest structures and visual paths based on your content, making complex ideas easier to follow.

Quick tips:
  • Great for storytelling and big‑picture concepts
  • AI suggests a flow for your topic
  • Ideal for student projects and interactive lessons

3 · Prompt Library for Writing & Presentations

A prompt is simply what you type into an AI tool to get a useful response. The better your prompt, the better your result. Think of it like giving directions — the clearer you are, the more likely you end up where you want to go. All of these prompts are ready to copy and paste. Just replace the parts in [brackets] with your specific details.

Where to use these prompts: ChatGPT, Google Gemini, or any AI writing tool work best for these.

Level:

Writing – Lesson Plans & Curriculum Materials

Lesson draft Basic
“Write a draft lesson plan for a [X]‑minute lesson on [Topic] for Grade [Y]. Include a hook, main activity, and a closing reflection.”
Learning objectives Intermediate
“Write 3 clear, measurable learning objectives for a [Grade] lesson on [Topic] using Bloom's taxonomy.”
Warm‑up Basic
“Suggest 3 engaging warm‑up activities for a [Grade] class on [Topic] that take 5 minutes each.”
Differentiation Intermediate
“Provide 3 differentiation strategies for a lesson on [Topic] for Grade [Y] to support below‑level, at‑level, and above‑level students.”
Materials list Basic
“List the materials and resources I need to teach a lesson on [Topic] for Grade [Y].”
Unit outline Advanced
“Design a 6‑week unit outline for [Subject] for Grade [Y], with essential questions, key assessments, and weekly learning goals.”

Writing – Professional Communication (Emails, Reports, Newsletters)

Parent email Basic
“Write a friendly, professional email to parents of Grade [Y] students about [Topic/Event]. Keep it positive and clear.”
Newsletter intro Basic
“Write a warm introduction for a class newsletter for Grade [Y] parents, highlighting what we covered this week and what's coming next.”
Progress report Intermediate
“Draft a progress report comment for a [Grade] student who [describe strengths and area for growth] in [Subject], using a supportive tone.”
Reminder Basic
“Write a short, clear reminder to parents about [event/deadline] for Grade [Y] with a friendly tone.”
Difficult conversation Intermediate
“Draft a script for a parent‑teacher meeting about a Grade [Y] student who is struggling with [issue], focusing on solutions and partnership.”
Annual report Advanced
“Write a draft of a grade‑level annual report summarising student progress, achievements, and goals for the next year, in a professional and encouraging tone.”

Writing – Student Feedback & Assessment

Positive feedback Basic
“Write 5 positive, specific comments I can give a Grade [Y] student who did well on [assignment/task].”
Constructive feedback Basic
“Write a constructive feedback comment for a Grade [Y] student's [project/essay] that highlights strengths and offers one clear next step.”
Rubric language Intermediate
“Write clear, student‑friendly descriptors for a rubric on [Topic/Assignment] for Grade [Y] across 4 levels of performance.”
Goal setting Intermediate
“Draft 3 learning goals for a Grade [Y] student in [Subject] based on their recent performance, with actionable steps for each.”
Portfolio reflection Advanced
“Write a reflection prompt and guiding questions for Grade [Y] students to use when reflecting on their learning portfolio for [Subject].”

Writing – Creative & Instructional Content

Writing prompts Basic
“Generate 10 creative writing prompts for Grade [Y] students on the theme [Theme].”
Explanations Basic
“Explain [Concept] in simple, easy‑to‑understand language for a Grade [Y] student. Use examples.”
Story starters Intermediate
“Write 5 story starters for Grade [Y] students that connect to our current topic [Topic].”
Project instructions Intermediate
“Write clear, step‑by‑step instructions for a Grade [Y] project on [Topic], including materials and success criteria.”
Cross‑curricular text Advanced
“Write a short, engaging informational text for Grade [Y] students that connects [Subject A] and [Subject B] through [Theme].”

Presentations – Slide Content & Outlines

Slide outline Basic
“Give me a slide‑by‑slide outline for a presentation on [Topic] for a [Grade] audience. Include key points per slide.”
Intro slide Basic
“Write an engaging introduction slide for a presentation on [Topic] for Grade [Y]. Include a hook and overview.”
Data slide Intermediate
“Suggest how to present [data/facts] on a slide for Grade [Y] in a clear, visual way. Write the accompanying talking points.”
Conclusion slide Basic
“Write a strong conclusion slide and call‑to‑action for a presentation on [Topic] for Grade [Y].”
Discussion questions Intermediate
“Generate 5 discussion questions for a presentation on [Topic] to engage a Grade [Y] class.”
Interactive elements Advanced
“Suggest 3 interactive elements (polls, Q&A, activities) to add to my presentation on [Topic] for Grade [Y] to boost engagement.”

Presentations – Speaker Notes & Scripts

Speaker notes Basic
“Write speaker notes for a [X]‑minute presentation on [Topic] for Grade [Y]. Keep the language natural and conversational.”
Opening script Basic
“Write an opening script (60 seconds) for my presentation on [Topic] that grabs attention and sets the context.”
Transition phrases Intermediate
“Give me 5 smooth transition phrases to move between sections in my presentation on [Topic].”
Storytelling script Intermediate
“Turn this presentation outline on [Topic] into a storytelling script that would engage a Grade [Y] audience.”
Q&A prep Advanced
“Anticipate 5 likely questions from a Grade [Y] class about [Topic] and draft concise, clear answers for each.”

Presentations – Design & Visuals

Image suggestions Basic
“Suggest 5 types of images or visuals I should include in a presentation on [Topic] for Grade [Y] and where to place them.”
Colour scheme Basic
“Recommend a colour scheme and font pairing for a presentation on [Topic] that is age‑appropriate and visually appealing.”
Infographic ideas Intermediate
“Suggest an infographic layout for presenting [data/concept] in a presentation for Grade [Y].”
Animation tips Intermediate
“Give me 3 ways to use animation or motion in my presentation on [Topic] to enhance understanding without distracting.”
Slide makeover Advanced
“I have this slide with too much text: [paste text]. Redesign it visually for a Grade [Y] audience, suggesting layout, visuals, and bullet structure.”

Cross‑cutting – Writing + Presenting

From text to slides Basic
“Turn this lesson plan [paste] into a 10‑slide presentation outline for Grade [Y], with slide titles and bullet points.”
Handout + slides Intermediate
“Based on this presentation [paste outline], create a one‑page student handout that summarises the key takeaways for Grade [Y].”
Integrated unit Advanced
“Design a 2‑week unit for Grade [Y] on [Topic] that includes: a writing assignment, a presentation project, a rubric, and a peer feedback form.”

Note: These prompts work great in ChatGPT, Gemini, or any AI writing tool. For presentation tools, paste the generated outline and let the tool build the slides.

A note before you go

You don't need to master all of these tools at once. Pick one writing tool and one presentation tool, spend a week with them, and see how they fit into your routine. The teachers who get the most out of AI are the ones who start small and build from there. You've already taken the first step by reading this guide.
